PR Analysis for UNITED Airlines¶

The first set of plots show tweets frequency for AA and UA incidents. From the time the first UA video is uploaded, till the time twitter starts showing reactions (April 10th, noon-ish), UA does not take any meaningful action to calm down the situation. Even worst, at 12:27pm, the CEO, Munoz issued a statement, hoping he would smooth things over with a superficial apology about the re-accommodation incident. His words fueled the internet fire and things got out of control. Later on, at 7:30pm, United issued an internal memo to employees and blamed the victim for what happened. After some delay, the memo found its way to the twitter, and again the reactions are visible. Finally, on April 11th at 3:10 pm, CEO Munoz apologized and promised to make it right. It is very interesting to see that twitter activity drops significantly within few hours of the apology.

Now on the American Airlines recent incident, from the time the first video emerged to the time that AA issued a sincere apology and took responsibility was about 8 hours. Twitter activity shows drastic difference between this incident and the UA incident. At the end, AA PR management contained the issue and it never became a national news or a crisis.
