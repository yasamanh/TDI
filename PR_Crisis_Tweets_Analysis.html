Twitter, Facebook and other social media platforms are a necessity in every organization to build a successful relationship with customers. Companies use these platforms to engage with their customers and promote their product and services. However, as a result of this unprecedented direct access, Public Relations (PR) management has significantly changed. In the event of a PR issue or disaster, customers now expect a swift, open and honest response from the respective organization through social media.

In recent years, some companies were able to avert a PR crisis by quickly and openly addressing the problem. For example, when Southwest flight 345 had a landing incident at LaGuardia airport, people received the news within minutes on Twitter and were able to follow the situation through frequent updates provided by Southwest. On the other side of the spectrum, the recent United Airlines (UA) incident in which a customer was forcefully dragged out of an airplane and the airline reaction was an example of extremely poor PR management. Within a day the issue escalated to a full blown PR crisis.

In this project, I will analyze some recent PR issues, such as the the United Airlines and American Airline (AA) incidents. My plan is to find insight by looking at tweets crawled from twitter website. I will explore the United Airlines incident and compare it to the similar American Airlines incident that happened a couple of weeks later but did not create a PR crisis.

Analysis of Tweets Frequency and Events:¶

I use frequency of tweets that have mentions of United Airlines or American Airlines as one metric for good or poor PR management. A good management plan should be capable of preventing an incident from going viral (i.e., large number of tweets or “high frequency”). A poor PR plan will not bring the frequency down, and might even make the situation worse. To get a better insight of a company PR plan, I show the timeline of the company’s reactions on the tweet frequency plot, to be able to correlate reactions with outcome. At this stage, I assume that the overall sentiment of tweets in these type of situation are negative. I plan to show this fact using sentiment analysis later in my capstone project.

The first set of plots (Plots #1,#2,#3) show tweets frequency for AA and UA incidents. From the time the first UA video is uploaded, till the time twitter starts showing reactions (April 10th, noon-ish), UA does not take any meaningful action to calm down the situation (Plot #2). Even worst, at 12:27pm, the CEO, Munoz issued a statement, hoping he would smooth things over with a superficial apology about the “re-accommodation” incident. His words fueled the internet fire and things got out of control. Later on, at 7:30pm, United issued an internal memo to employees and blamed the victim for what happened. After some delay, the memo found its way to the twitter, and again the reactions are visible. Finally, on April 11th at 3:10 pm, CEO Munoz apologized and promised to “make it right.” It is very interesting to see that twitter activity drops significantly within few hours of the apology.

Now on the American Airlines recent incident, from the time the first video emerged to the time that AA issued a sincere apology and took responsibility was about 8 hours (Plot #3). Twitter activity shows drastic difference between this incident and the UA incident. At the end, AA PR management contained the issue and it never became a national news or a crisis.

Discovering Stories by Topic Modeling¶

I give an overview of stories covered in the twitter collection using topic modeling and clustering tweets together as stories. Steps to create clusters of tweets that are less noisy and more informative, including:

  • divided my twitter collection into time slices of one week.
  • Noise and common word removal
  • Tweet tokenizing using NLTK
  • TFIDF
  • Topic modeling(Latent Semantic Analysis)
  • added a probability threshold to further remove the noise tweets in each cluster.

def highlight_Stories(s):
    arr1 = s.index.get_level_values(1)=='Topic #1'

    is_highlight = arr1 & (arr2 | arr3)
    return ['background-color: yellow' if v else 'color: darkblue' for v in is_highlight]

s = pd_csv_indexed.style.apply(highlight_Stories)
